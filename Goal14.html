Goal 14: Life Below Water

Overview: Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development.

Key Targets:

  • Prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, in particular from land-based activities.
  • Protect and restore ecosystems and biodiversity in the oceans and seas.
  • Prohibit certain forms of fisheries subsidies that contribute to overcapacity and overfishing.
  • Increase scientific knowledge and develop research capacity on ocean acidification.
  • Enhance the conservation and sustainable use of oceans and their resources through international law.

Importance of Goal 14:

The oceans cover over 70% of the Earth's surface and are crucial for life on Earth. They regulate climate, support biodiversity, and provide livelihoods for millions of people. Protecting our oceans and ensuring their sustainable use is essential for the well-being of future generations and the health of our planet.
