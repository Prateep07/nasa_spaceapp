Goal 2: Zero Hunger

Overview: End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture.

Key Targets:

  • By 2030, end hunger and ensure access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food all year round for everyone, especially the poor and people in vulnerable situations.
  • End all forms of malnutrition, including achieving the internationally agreed targets on stunting and wasting in children under 5 years of age.
  • Double the agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers, particularly women, indigenous peoples, family farmers, and fishers.
  • Ensure sustainable food production systems and implement resilient agricultural practices that increase productivity and production.

Importance of Goal 2:

Goal 2 aims to ensure that no one is left behind in the fight against hunger and malnutrition. Hunger and food insecurity affect millions of people globally, reducing their ability to lead healthy, productive lives. Sustainable agriculture is crucial for food security and for reducing the negative impact on the environment.
