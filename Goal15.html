Goal 15: Life on Land

Overview: Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and biodiversity loss.

Key Targets:

  • Ensure the conservation of mountain ecosystems, including their biodiversity.
  • Combat desertification, restore degraded land and soil, and strive to achieve a land degradation-neutral world.
  • Ensure the sustainable management of forests, halt deforestation, restore degraded forests, and increase afforestation and reforestation.
  • Protect and prevent the extinction of threatened species.
  • Mobilize significant resources from all sources to finance sustainable forest management and biodiversity conservation.

Importance of Goal 15:

Land ecosystems are crucial for the survival of countless species, including humans. They provide essential services such as clean air and water, food production, and climate regulation. Protecting and restoring terrestrial ecosystems is vital for combating climate change, ensuring food security, and maintaining biodiversity, which in turn supports healthy ecosystems and human well-being.
